## 1. What Convoyar is (and is not)

**Convoyar IS:**

- A tool to **coordinate** shared rides.
- A place to **organize** trips in private groups (organizations, clubs, schools,
  events) and to **publish or request a seat** in public trips.
- Software that suggests who drives whom and at which meeting point.

**Convoyar is NOT:**

- A transportation, ride-hailing, taxi, or shuttle company.
- The carrier or the trip organizer: **the trip is done by people**, not by
  Convoyar.
- An insurer or a verifier of drivers, vehicles, or passengers.

Convoyar connects people who want to share a ride. What happens during that ride
is the responsibility of those who take part.

## 5. How ride coordination works

- You create or join a **trip/event** with an origin, a destination, and a time.
- Convoyar suggests **assignments** (who drives whom) respecting limits such as
  vehicle capacity, detour, time window, maximum walk, and declared needs.
- To protect your privacy, **computed meeting points** are shared, not your exact
  home address.
- Suggestions are **guidance only**. The final decision to travel, with whom, and
  under what conditions is **always yours and the other people's**.

## 6. Contributions between users (not for profit)

Carpooling is about **sharing costs**, not making money. If the app shows a
**suggested contribution** (for example, to cover fuel), it is purely
**informational** and aimed at splitting the trip's real costs.

`[REVIEW WITH A LAWYER]` Convoyar **does not process payments** between users. Any
financial arrangement happens directly between people. In many countries,
charging above the shared cost can turn a trip into commercial transport, with
obligations (licenses, insurance, taxes) that fall solely on the driver. We do
not promote or enable for-profit transportation.
